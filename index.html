The Lean/Agile Transformation

  • I was involved in a large departmental lean and agile transformation in an unnamed bank

Transformation context

  • Large department, 400 or so developers, London based as well as offshore
  • Previous offshoring had gutted the institutional knowledge
  • High tech debt
  • Low autonomy
  • High attrition

Transformation vision

  • Wipe the slate clean
  • Vision dictated by a full business engagement (not just IT!)
  • Change the product ownership model, specifications and technical practices
  • Leadership from all levels (Turn the Ship Around)

Assembling the coaching team

  • External expert coaches
  • Technical practices coach (me!)
  • Lean specialists

Setting the transformation goals

  • Improve the effectiveness of teams, through
    • better teamwork
    • better skills
  • Increase happiness
  • Reduce the lead time

Too many cooks

  • First problem: unclear leadership
  • Two senior leaders could agree to a clear set of priorities

Not enough cooks

  • Second problem: missing leadership
  • Transferred to other departments, unable to contribute and provide top cover

Stop distracting everyone

  • Third problem: stop distracting people, we've got to hit deadlines!
  • Annual planning cycle had put too much work in our backlog

Time to leave...

  • I exited here
  • Senior support evaporated, unable to negotiate on deadlines
  • Management didn't want to change (heros who have been promoted)

What is Culture?

  • Many definitions

Whatever people learn from other people, and transmit to other people, especially by interacting with them

  • Embodied culture is the things we don’t event need to think about
  • Values and goals that guide our actions
  • Habitus: many of our habits are acquired as part of the culture in which we are immersed

The 4 Forces of Culture

  • When thinking about changing culture, it helps to understand the various forces working on culture

1. Intertial Force

  • Laws of motion:
    • an object at rest wants to stay at rest
    • an object in motion wants to keep to the same speed and direction
  • Existential interia (story about cutting the ends of the meat)
  • Habitual intertia (e.g. accent and speaking, default set of tools we use

2. Entropic Force

  • the tendency towards disorder in the transmission of culture
  • works against inertia

3. Reflective (Metacultural) Force

  • metaculture is culture that is about (or reflects upon) other culture.
  • e.g. laws, policies, rules
  • New rules and policies can be created by those in power or with influence
  • Can act to both keep things the same, or as an agent of change

4. The Force of Interest

  • these are produced by the feelings and emotions
  • a good leader understands the emotional responses to cultural elements, and uses this to affect change
  • Incremental new-ness can a positive attractive force. (e.g. new model of a car every year)
  • Force of interest can either work to keep culture static, or it could drive culture to change. It’s at the heart of successful culture change

All the forces:

  1. Intertial Force
  2. Entropic Force
  3. Reflective (Metacultural) Force
  4. The Force of Interest
  • All the forces can work with each other, or in opposition
  • Successful change involves harnessing all the forces towards the desired outcome

The Secret to Successful Culture Change

the new culture must appear to grow organically out of the old culture
  • It must be new enough to be recognisable as different, but also sufficiently similar that its relationship to the old culture can be sensed.
  • Feelings or affections can propel the motion of culture, or inhibit it. These feelings are hugely influential.
